History of Metaverse?

Brief History of Metaverse The term Metaverse is derived from the Greek words 'meta' (beyond) and 'verse' (universe). Consider it a universe beyond reality that will be capable of replacing every activity in our lives. The word "metaverse" originally appears in Neal Stephenson's 1992 science-fiction novel Snowcrash, which depicts a future in which millions of individuals interact in a cyberspace environment using virtual avatars. This notion was popularized further in Earnest Cline's 2011 sci-fi novel Ready Player One, in which ordinary individuals strap on VR headsets and log into a virtual world to play out their imaginations. Later in the early 1990s, a firm called Sega launched VR gaming devices, such as the SEGA VR-1 motion circular, which aimed to provide exciting experiences for arcade gamers.
